Cities

Italy is home to some of the most iconic cities in the world. You've got Rome, the Eternal City, steeped in history and architecture like the Colosseum and Pantheon. Then there's Florence, the birthplace of the Renaissance, where art and culture flow through the streets like the Arno River. Venice, the City of Water, is a maze of canals and romance. From Milan's high-end fashion to Naples' pizza and coastal charm, Italy's cities each have their own vibe. Bologna's medieval architecture and food scene are often overlooked but totally worth a visit. And let's not forget cities like Turin, with its royal heritage, or Palermo, rich in Sicilian culture. Italy's got a city for every kind of traveler.

Events

Italy is known for its vibrant festivals and events that showcase its rich culture and history. You've got Carnevale di Venezia, one of the most famous carnivals in the world, where masks and elaborate costumes take over the streets of Venice. Then there's the Opera Festival in Verona, where classical performances are held in an ancient amphitheater. The Festa dei Nozze d'Oro in Florence is another big deal, celebrating the city's patron saint, St. John the Baptist. From music to food, Italy's events calendar is packed. The Il Palio di Siena horse race is a thrilling experience, while the Vinitaly wine festival in Verona is a must for wine lovers. If you're into film, the Venice Film Festival is one of the oldest and most prestigious in the world. And let's not forget the Christmas markets and Easter celebrations that bring communities together across the country.
